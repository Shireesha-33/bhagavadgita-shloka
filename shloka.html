SHLOKA

"यस्य सर्वे समारम्भा: कामसङ्कल्पवर्जिता: |
ज्ञानाग्निदग्धकर्माणं तमाहु: पण्डितं बुधा: || 19||"

FEMALE AUDIO

MALE AUDIO

MEANING

Meaning: The enlightened sages call those persons wise, whose every action is free from the desire for material pleasures and who have burnt the reactions of work in the fire of divine knowledge.

WORD TO WORD

yasya—whose; sarve—every; samārambhāḥ—undertakings; kāma—desire for material pleasures; saṅkalpa—resolve; varjitāḥ—devoid of; jñāna—divine knowledge; agni—in the fire; dagdha—burnt; karmāṇam—actions; tam—him; āhuḥ—address; paṇḍitam—a sage; budhāḥ—the wise

SUMMARY

The soul, being a divine spark of blissful God, naturally seeks joy. However, when encased in the physical body, the soul identifies itself with the body and seeks happiness through material actions. These actions, aimed at sensory pleasures, bind the soul to the cycle of karma. But when the soul attains spiritual knowledge, it realizes that true happiness is not found in material objects but through loving service to God. It dedicates all actions to God, as expressed in the Bhagavad Gita (9.27). This transforms worldly actions into divine offerings, freeing the soul from karmic bondage.
